Banking on Behavior: How I Used R to Decode Customer Value

R | Customer Segmentation | RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)

November, 2024 Serena Fang
The goal: figure out which customers bring the most value‚Äîand how to tailor rewards and retention strategies to each group.

The Business Question
The bank wanted to sharpen its loyalty rewards program. Not everyone should get premium perks‚Äîand guessing isn‚Äôt a strategy. We needed a reliable, data-driven way to:
‚Ä¢ Rank customers by value

The Dataset: 6 Months, 8,900 Customers
We had six months of behavioral data for 8,900 active credit card holders. Each row was a customer, with variables like:

  • PURCHASES: total dollar amount spent
  • PURCHASES_TRX: number of transactions
  • CREDIT_LIMIT: their credit cap
  • PAYMENTS: what they actually paid
  • MINIMUM_PAYMENTS: what they had to pay

The Framework: RFM, But Customized
RFM usually stands for Recency, Frequency, Monetary. I adapted it to reflect banking behavior:

Metric What It Captures Formula
Activity How often they transact PURCHASES_TRX
Spending How much credit they use PURCHASES / CREDIT_LIMIT
Discipline How responsibly they pay PAYMENTS / MINIMUM_PAYMENTS

Using R, I grouped customers into clear, actionable clusters: 1. Loyal High-Value Customers
‚Ä¢ High activity, high spending, high payment discipline

‚Ä¢ Decent activity and spending, solid discipline
‚Ä¢ With a little nudge (targeted offers), they could move up. 3. Occasional High Spenders
‚Ä¢ Spend big but transact rarely.
‚Ä¢ A valuable segment for big-ticket promotions. 4. High Spenders with Risk
‚Ä¢ Spend a lot, often‚Äîbut barely meet payment minimums
‚Ä¢ Red flag. May require credit limit reviews or intervention.
